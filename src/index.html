Frequently Asked Questions

Need help casting your vote? Here are some helpful links

Am I eligible?


Any resident of Ohio who is not currently incarcerated is eligible to vote if they are registered. This includes homeless people, people with disabilities, people with felony convictions, and non-English speakers. If you have not voted recently, you may need to register again

Check registration

How can I register?


To vote, you must be registered. If you have never voted before, or you find that you are no longer registered, you can print out a registration form and mail it in. If you need help filling out the form, feel free to contact us for help.

Registration form

How can I get to the polls?


That's where we come in! We have cars and drivers standing by to take you to the polling place on election day. Fill out the form below and we'll contact you to arrange a ride.

Get a ride

What will I need to vote?


In order to vote in Ohio, you may need to provide proof of identity. This is commonly a driver's license or other photo ID card, but if you don't have one, you can also use a utility bill, paycheck, or bank statement.

Where is my polling place?


In order to vote, you will need to turn up at your assigned polling place. We can get you there, but we need to know where to take you. You can look up your polling place at the link below:
